Birthplace of Buddha

About

Welcome to Lumbini, the sacred birthplace of Buddha and one of the most revered pilgrimage sites in the world. Located in the Rupandehi District of Nepal, Lumbini holds immense significance for millions of Buddhists worldwide. According to tradition, it was here, in the 6th century BCE, that Queen Maya Devi gave birth to Prince Siddhartha Gautama under the shade of a Bodhi tree. As the founder of Buddhism and known as the "Enlightened One," Buddha's teachings have shaped the spiritual and philosophical landscape of humanity. Today, Lumbini stands as a tranquil and spiritually enriching destination, encompassing numerous monasteries, temples, and archaeological remains that offer visitors a glimpse into the life and legacy of one of history's most influential figures.
